**User Goal:**
- Learn how to self-evaluate card condition BEFORE paying for PSA grading

3. **"Complete 2025 Guide"** = User expects comprehensive, up-to-date information

### User Journey Stage:
**Middle of Funnel** - User has cards, knows about PSA, wants to evaluate before committing to cost

## Format Decision

### ✅ CHOSEN FORMAT: **Single Comprehensive Educational Guide** (Pillar Page)

**Rationale:**
1. **Intent Match:** Users want ONE authoritative resource for self-grading, not scattered pages
2. **Topical Authority:** A single deep guide > multiple shallow pages
3. **User Experience:** Step-by-step flow works better in one page vs. cluster
4. **Conversion:** Keep users on one page to build trust before CTA
5. **SERP Pattern:** Top results are single long-form guides (4,000+ words)

### Content Structure:

#### **Main Sections:**

1. **Introduction**
   - Why pre-grading matters (save money, set expectations)
   - What you'll learn in this guide
   - Quick checklist preview

2. **Understanding PSA Grading Criteria** (Foundation)
   - The 4 key factors: Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface
   - PSA grading scale explained (1-10)
   - Visual examples of each grade

3. **Tools & Equipment You Need**
   - Magnifying lamp/loupe (10x recommended)
   - Card centering tool (or DIY method)
   - Penny sleeves and card savers
   - White gloves
   - Good lighting setup
   - Optional: digital calipers, UV light

4. **Step-by-Step Self-Grading Process** (CORE VALUE)
   - **Step 1:** Clean your hands & workspace
   - **Step 2:** Initial visual inspection (any obvious flaws?)
   - **Step 3:** Centering measurement (how to measure, acceptable ratios)
   - **Step 4:** Corner inspection (magnified examination technique)
   - **Step 5:** Edge inspection (what to look for)
   - **Step 6:** Surface inspection (front and back)
   - **Step 7:** Assign a preliminary grade
   - **Step 8:** Decision: Send to PSA or not?

5. **Common Self-Grading Mistakes to Avoid**
   - Overlooking minor surface issues
   - Misjudging centering
   - Not checking under magnification
   - Ignoring the back of the card
   - Confirmation bias (wanting a higher grade)

6. **Which Cards Are Worth Grading?**
   - Decision tree flowchart
   - Value threshold calculator concept
   - Vintage vs. modern considerations
   - Rookie cards and key cards

7. **Comparing Your Grade to PSA Results**
   - Expected variance (your PSA 9 might be PSA 8)
   - Why professional grading differs
   - Case studies: Self-grade vs. actual PSA grade

8. **Preparing Cards for PSA Submission** (Transition to action)
   - How to package correctly
   - Service level selection
   - Submission form tips
   - Insurance recommendations

9. **FAQ Section** (20+ questions)
   - Can I grade cards as accurately as PSA?
   - What magnification do PSA graders use?
   - How do I measure centering without a tool?
   - What's the most common mistake beginners make?
   - Should I crack and regrade?
   - Etc.

10. **Downloadable Resources**
    - Printable grading checklist PDF
    - Centering ratio cheat sheet
    - Decision flowchart

11. **Next Steps**
    - CTA to PreGrade tool (if applicable)
    - Link to PSA submission guide
    - Newsletter signup
