## Strategy 4: FAQ Optimization for AI

### Anticipate AI Query Patterns

**Problem:** AI answers questions directly  
**Solution:** FAQ schema with complete answers

**Our FAQ Strategy (10 Questions):**

1. **"What are the most common hidden defects in PSA grading?"**
   > "The most common hidden defects that prevent PSA 10 grades are: 1) Print lines (40% of rejections), 2) Silvering (30%), 3) Holo scratches (20%), and 4) Soft corners (10%). These defects are often invisible to the naked eye but detected under PSA's magnified inspection."

2. **"How can I spot print lines on Pokemon cards?"**
   > "To spot print lines: 1) Use a 10x jeweler's loupe, 2) Hold the card at a 45-degree angle under bright light, 3) Look for horizontal or vertical lines across the surface, especially in solid color areas. Print lines appear as thin, consistent lines from the factory printing process."

3. **"What is silvering in card grading?"**
   > "Silvering is edge whitening caused by handling or storage. To detect: 1) Examine all four edges under bright light, 2) Look for white or light-colored areas along the card edge, 3) Check back edges (PSA allows 75/25 centering, but no silvering). Even minor silvering can prevent PSA 10."

4. **"Can I fix card defects before PSA submission?"**
   > "No. PSA prohibits any alteration or restoration. Attempting to fix defects (pressing, coloring edges, etc.) will result in rejection and potential ban. If your card has defects, submit with accurate expectations or hold the card."

5. **"What tools do I need to inspect cards for grading?"**
   > "Essential tools: 1) 10x jeweler's loupe ($10-20), 2) 5000K LED light source ($15-30), 3) Cotton gloves ($5), 4) Clean surface. Optional: Black light for UV damage ($15). Total investment: $30-70, pays for itself after one prevented bad submission."

6. **"What's the difference between soft corners and hard corners?"**
   > "Soft corners are rounded from handling/manufacturing, while hard corners are sharp and crisp. To check: 1) Use 10x loupe, 2) Examine corner point, 3) Look for rounding or 'softness' vs. sharp 90-degree angle. Even slight rounding prevents PSA 10."

7. **"How strict is PSA on factory defects vs handling damage?"**
   > "PSA's official stance: 'A Gem Mint 10 is virtually perfect with only minor printing imperfections allowed if not visible from 16 inches away.' Factory defects (print lines, roller marks) are treated the same as handling damage (scratches, dents) for PSA 10 consideration."

8. **"Should I submit my card if I find a hidden defect?"**
   > "Decision matrix: 1) If defect is minor and card value is high (>$500 PSA 9 value), consider submitting with PSA 8-9 expectation, 2) If defect is moderate or card value is low (<$100), hold and save submission cost, 3) If uncertain, use PreGrade service for professional evaluation."

9. **"What is centering tolerance for PSA 10?"**
   > "PSA 10 centering requirements: Front = 55/45 to 60/40 (max), Back = 75/25 (max). To measure: 1) Use ruler or centering tool, 2) Measure border width on opposite sides, 3) Calculate ratio. Example: 3mm vs 2mm = 60/40. Cards outside this tolerance cannot achieve PSA 10."

10. **"Can hidden defects be seen in regular photos?"**
    > "No. Hidden defects require: 1) Magnification (10x loupe), 2) Specific lighting angles (45-degree), and 3) Close inspection. Regular photos miss: print lines, micro-chipping, soft corners, light silvering. This is why in-person inspection before submission is critical."
