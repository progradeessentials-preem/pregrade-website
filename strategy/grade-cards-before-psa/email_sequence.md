**Surface flaws are the HARDEST to self-assess.**

PSA has professional-grade lighting and magnification equipment. They see things we can't see with basic tools.

But here's their technique you can replicate at home:

**The Angled Light Method:**

1. Place card flat on your work surface
2. Position LED lamp directly overhead (reveals print lines)
3. Then angle the light at 45 degrees (reveals scratches)
4. Rotate the card slowly under the angled light
5. Use 10x loupe to inspect every quadrant

**Why the angled light matters:**

Scratches are nearly invisible under direct lighting. But when you angle the light, they create tiny reflections that are easy to spot.

I've caught surface flaws with this method that I completely missed with overhead lighting alone.

**Common Surface Issues to Look For:**
- Print lines (horizontal lines - factory defect)
- Micro-scratches (from penny sleeves or handling)
- Surface scuffs (cloudy areas)
- Staining (especially wax staining on vintage cards)

Remember: Check BOTH the front AND back. The back counts just as much.

**Tools You Need:**
- 10x magnifying loupe: $15-30 on Amazon

- Grading fee: $75 (Express service because I was excited)

- Net loss: $105

What went wrong?

**Confirmation Bias.**

I wanted it to be a PSA 10 SO badly that I convinced myself every flaw was "acceptable" or "not that bad."

When I got it back, I re-inspected it properly:
- Centering: 72/28 front (way off for PSA 10)
- Corners: Three sharp, one SLIGHTLY soft (I missed this)
- Surface: Tiny print line on back (invisible without loupe + good lighting)

ALL of these were detectable before submission.

But I saw what I wanted to see, not what was actually there.

**The Lesson:**

Be brutally honest during inspection. Use the checklist systematically. Don't skip steps because you're excited.

If a card fails ANY of the 4 criteria, it's not a PSA 10.

**Better Approach:**

1. Use the checklist for EVERY card
2. Measure centering (don't eyeball)
3. Use 10x magnification for corners and surface
4. Inspect both front AND back thoroughly
5. Be honest about flaws

**Q1: Can I use a regular magnifying glass instead of a jeweler's loupe?**

A: Yes, as long as it's 10x magnification. PSA uses 10x, so you need to match that. Most drugstore magnifying glasses are 3x-5x, which isn't enough. Look for "10x jeweler's loupe" on Amazon ($15-30).

A: Use a millimeter ruler:
- Measure left border: 3mm
- Measure right border: 5mm
- Ratio: 3:5 = 60/40
